Key Mathematical Findings

  • Sigmoidal Learning Curve: Win% follows a logistic function — slow early exploration, rapid mid-phase climb, asymptotic ceiling near 95%.
  • ε Piecewise Decay: Epsilon decays linearly until Ep ~2100 (slope ≈ −4.5×10⁻⁴ per ep), then hard-floors at 0.010 (minimum exploration rate).
  • Policy Polarity Flip: Snake reward transitions from consistently negative (losing) to consistently positive (winning) between Ep 1500–1900, coinciding with ε dropping below 0.20.
  • Steps Bimodality: Steps distribution is bimodal — a high-step mode (timeouts ~200, seen Ep 1600 & 1800) and a low-step convergent mode (~20–30), indicating the agent found efficient winning strategies.
  • Loss Regime Shift: Loss jumps from ~0.03–0.07 (early) to a sustained ~0.15–0.40 plateau post Ep 2100 — characteristic of a Q-value overestimation or high TD-error variance once policy is mostly greedy.

Anomaly Detection & Notable Events

  • Ep 600: First positive Snake reward (+10.30) — an outlier during early exploration; likely a lucky episode, not a stable strategy yet.
  • Ep 700: Reverts to −12.19 (worst reward overall) and high steps (150). Classic "2-steps forward, 1-step back" in RL.
  • Ep 1600 & 1800: Steps hit 200 (max cap). The agent appears to have entered a near-draw/loop scenario — timeout. Partial rewards (−2.79, −2.89) confirm incomplete episodes.
  • Ep 2100: ε reaches 0.030 (near-greedy). Loss spikes to 0.3626 — highest until this point. Q-value bootstrapping instability as exploration collapses.
  • Ep 2500+: Win rate stabilises at 92–97% with low steps (14–69). Agent has converged to efficient, reliable policy.
  • Ep 3100–3200: Loss spikes (0.3986, 0.3747) — possible replay buffer instability or Q-value divergence spike.

Epsilon Decay Mathematical Analysis

The ε schedule follows a multiplicative (exponential) decay model: εₙ = ε₀ · γⁿ where ε₀ = 1.0, and γ is the per-episode decay rate.


From the data: at Ep 100, ε ≈ 0.964. At Ep 2000, ε ≈ 0.069. Solving: γ = (0.069/0.964)^(1/1900) ≈ 0.998755 per episode.

Half-life of exploration: t₁/₂ = ln(0.5)/ln(0.998755) ≈ 556 episodes — meaning 50% of exploration capacity is lost by Ep ~556.

At ε = 0.010 (floor), the agent acts greedily ~99% of the time. The exploration budget is exhausted by Ep ~2050, which precisely matches when win rates exceed 85% and stabilise.

Loss Dynamics & Training Stability

Loss (TD-error / Huber loss) exhibits two distinct regimes:


  • Phase I–III (Ep 100–2000): Mean loss = 0.044, σ = 0.029. Low and relatively stable — agent is learning from diverse exploratory experience. The replay buffer is well-mixed.
  • Phase IV (Ep 2100–4000): Mean loss = 0.198, σ = 0.082. Approximately 4.5× higher. This is a known DQN phenomenon: as ε → ε_min, the replay buffer becomes increasingly dominated by on-policy (greedy) trajectories, reducing diversity and causing correlated updates → higher TD variance.

Despite elevated loss, performance is unaffected — win rate is 90–97%. This suggests the high loss is not from policy degradation but from high-value states being revisited with bootstrapped targets that shift as Q-values sharpen. A sign of healthy convergence, not divergence.
